01
Identity & Values
What Lextech stands for, what it promises, and why it exists. This section is the foundation everything else is built on.

Lextech is not just another dev shop. It is a technology partner — clients come to you not just to build a website but to solve business problems using technology. You consult, you build, you support, and you grow alongside them. That positioning makes you sticky and hard to replace.

Mission
"To power Ghanaian businesses with technology that actually works — practical, affordable, and built to grow with them."
Vision
"To be the most trusted technology partner for businesses across Ghana and West Africa — the company people call when they want it done right."

"We don't sell software. We sell outcomes. The software is just how we deliver them."

Core Values

Reliability first
We do what we say, when we say it. Clients and teammates must be able to count on us absolutely. Reliability is the brand before any service is.
Build for the client
Every decision starts with the client's outcome, not our convenience or what's technically interesting. The client's win is our win.
Grow each other
Knowledge is shared freely. A stronger teammate means a stronger Lextech. We teach, we mentor, we lift. No knowledge gatekeeping.
Own your work
Every person takes full responsibility for their output — its quality, its timeline, and its impact. No blame-shifting, ever.
Simplicity over complexity
We solve problems in the simplest way that works. Elegant, not complicated. Practical, not impressive. Our clients should understand what we build.
Radical honesty
We tell clients, teammates, and ourselves the truth — even when it is uncomfortable. No sugar-coating, no hiding problems, no false promises.

The Lextech Promise

To clients

We will solve your problem, not just complete your brief. You get a partner, not a vendor. We are still here after the project ends and we care about your results.

To the team

We will grow together. Your skills, your income, and your career grow as Lextech grows. This is not a side gig — it is a career worth betting on.

To Ghana

We build technology that works for our context — affordable, reliable, designed for how Ghanaians actually do business. Not copy-pasted from Silicon Valley.

To ourselves

We will not cut corners, ship poor work, or compromise our integrity for a quick win. Quality is the brand and our long-term reputation is worth protecting.

Don't offer everything at once. Clients trust specialists. "Lextech builds great software for Ghanaian businesses" is a stronger pitch than "we do everything tech."

Building & Keeping Your Team
Loyalty is built through fairness, growth, and belonging — not just money. Your early team members are co-builders, not employees.

The team you build in your first year will define what Lextech becomes. Hire slowly, invest heavily in the people you choose, and create a culture that makes talented people want to stay even when they get better offers.

Retention Package

Arranged from what you offer immediately to what you build toward. Be transparent with your team about what is available now and what is coming.

Offer immediately
Claude Team Subscription
Gives every member a serious AI advantage in their daily work. Shows that Lextech invests in their productivity and positions the company as forward-thinking from day one.
Offer immediately
Data / Internet Stipend
GHS 100–200/month per person. Removes a real daily friction point. Extremely high perceived value relative to its cost — it lands every single month and is never forgotten.
Months 1–3
Skills & Certification Budget
GHS 500–1,000/year per person for Udemy, Coursera, Google, and AWS certifications. They grow, you benefit. Their certification is Lextech's credential too.
Months 1–3
Documented Growth Path
Show each person their title, salary, and responsibility trajectory for 18–24 months. Ambiguity about the future kills loyalty faster than low pay ever could.
Months 1–3
Profit-Sharing Commitment
Commit 10–15% of net profit to a team pool once revenue starts. Document it formally. Even a small first distribution changes how people think and talk about their work.
Months 3–6
Flexible Work Arrangements
Hybrid schedule where the work allows it. Autonomy is one of the most valued benefits among young Ghanaian tech talent — and it costs Lextech almost nothing.
Months 3–6
Monthly Team Events
Team lunch, outing, or shared meal. Community feeling is your single biggest competitive advantage over large firms. Use it. These moments create loyalty money cannot buy.
Year 1+
Phantom Equity / ESOP
Small percentage ownership for founding team members. Makes them owners in spirit and eventually in law. Creates the deepest possible loyalty — they are building something of their own.
Year 1+
Health Insurance
Even a basic NHIS top-up plan signals that you care about team members as humans, not just workers. This single benefit earns enormous goodwill and trust.
Year 1+
Conference & Event Attendance
Send team members to tech conferences, hackathons, and industry events. They come back energized, inspired, and deeply loyal to the company that invested in their growth.

Where to Find Loyal Founding Members

Don't hire — recruit. Hiring is reactive. Recruiting means actively looking for the right person before you desperately need them. Here is where to find them in Ghana:

  • University tech communities — KNUST, UG, Ashesi, and GCTU all have active developer groups, hackathon clubs, and CS associations. These students are hungry, talented, and not yet hardened by corporate culture.
  • Ghana Developer Communities — GDG Accra, Developers In Vogue, Python Ghana, local WhatsApp tech groups. Show up at events, give value first, then recruit. Relationships before everything.
  • Referrals from your own network — Your first 3 members will almost certainly come from people you already know or trust. A trusted person vouching for someone is worth more than any CV.
  • Ghanaian freelancers on Upwork or Fiverr — Find local talent who are looking for stability. They already have proven skills, a work ethic, and client management experience.
  • LinkedIn — Post honestly about what you are building. "Join me to build the best tech company in Ghana" beats a boring job ad. The right people will find you if your story is compelling.

"Look for attitude first, skill second. You can train skills. You cannot train someone to care, to be honest, or to show up. A curious, reliable junior beats an arrogant senior every time."

The interview question that matters most: "Where do you want to be in 3 years, and what are you doing right now to get there?" Hire only the people who have a real, specific, honest answer.

Getting Clients in Ghana
The Ghanaian market rewards relationships, results, and referrals — in that order. Cold outreach alone will not build Lextech. You must be known, trusted, and recommended.

Who to Target First

Start with clients who have clear pain points, can pay, and will tell others about you. Every satisfied client in Ghana knows at least 10 other business owners they talk to regularly.

Start here
SMEs & Traders
Shops, distributors, small manufacturers. Need websites, inventory software, WhatsApp automation. Pay quickly and have many business-owner peers to refer you to.
Start here
Schools & Tutoring Centers
Need fee management systems, student portals, and professional websites. Multiple decision-makers but repeat long-term contracts once you're trusted inside.
Start here
Healthcare — Clinics
Appointment booking, patient records, billing. Willing to pay for reliability and support. A clinic recommends you to every other clinic they know and trust.
Grow into
Real Estate Firms
Property listing websites, CRM systems, automated WhatsApp lead follow-up. Fast-growing sector in Accra and Kumasi with high technology adoption appetite.
Grow into
Logistics & Delivery
Tracking dashboards, driver management, dispatch tools. Fast-growing post-pandemic sector with high digitization needs and little local software competition.
Grow into
NGOs & Development Orgs
Real budgets, need websites and reporting tools, and pay reliably and on time. Significantly easier to close than private corporations. Great for steady early revenue.
Long-term
Banks & Fintechs
High budgets but very slow procurement processes. Target after you have 10+ strong case studies and a verifiable track record of delivered projects.
Long-term
Government / MDAs
Large contracts but slow 90–180 day payment cycles. Plan cash flow carefully before pursuing these. Very valuable for Lextech's credibility and portfolio.

How to Approach Clients

01
Lead with problems, not services
Never walk in saying "we build websites." Walk in saying "I noticed your business has no way for customers to find you online — we have fixed that for 5 similar businesses and I want to show you how." Problem-first always wins in Ghana and anywhere.
02
Use warm introductions wherever possible
Referrals close 3x faster than cold outreach. Ask every happy client: "Do you know 2 business owners who might face similar challenges?" Most will say yes if you simply ask directly and genuinely.
03
Do one free or reduced thing to get in the door
Offer a free website audit, a free IT assessment, or a discounted first project. Once you are trusted inside a company, the next contract is easy. Your first 5 clients are your portfolio — invest in them generously even at a loss.
04
Show local case studies, not foreign examples
Ghanaian clients want to see that you have worked with Ghanaian businesses in conditions they recognize. A "we helped a shop in Spintex" story beats a Silicon Valley example every single time. Build your local portfolio fast.
05
Follow up relentlessly but professionally
Most Ghanaian deal cycles are slow. "I will think about it" almost always means "remind me in a week." Use a simple CRM and follow up every 5–7 days until you get a clear yes or a definitive no. Professional persistence is respected.
06
Always present three pricing tiers
Basic, Standard, and Premium. Most clients pick the middle option. This makes your premium seem reasonable and your basic look accessible. Never give a single price — you will always leave money on the table or lose the deal entirely.
07
Build partnerships that feed you referrals
Partner with business consultants, accountants, computer hardware shops, and digital agencies. They serve your exact target clients every single day. A 10% referral fee to an accountant who sends you 5 clients a year pays back in weeks.

"Your reputation in Ghana travels faster than any advertisement. One raving client is worth more than a six-month marketing campaign. Over-deliver on every early project — always."

Revenue Model

Project Fees
One-time payment for builds. Good for cash flow. Always collect 50% upfront and 50% on delivery. No exceptions — this rule protects Lextech from the beginning.
Monthly Retainers
Ongoing support, updates, and hosting management. Your most stable income stream. Aggressively convert every project client into a retainer after delivery.

05
Code of Conduct
The rules everyone at Lextech lives by — team members, contractors, and the founder. These only work if they are lived, not just printed. Review them as a team every quarter.

The 10 Non-Negotiables

I
Deliver or communicate — never disappear
If you cannot meet a deadline, say so before it passes, not after. Silence is the fastest way to destroy client and team trust alike. One honest message sent early prevents an entire crisis and preserves the relationship.
II
Own your mistakes completely
No blame-shifting, no excuses, no hiding. When something goes wrong, lead with: "Here is what happened and here is exactly how I am fixing it." Clients and teammates respect accountability far more than perfection. Own it fully.
III
Client data is sacred
No client information is shared externally, discussed casually, or used for any other purpose. This is both a legal and an ethical obligation. One breach ends Lextech's reputation permanently. Treat all client data as if it were your own private information.
IV
Teach, don't gatekeep
Hoarding knowledge to feel irreplaceable is toxic to any team. At Lextech, if you know something your teammate needs, you share it proactively. We grow together or we do not grow at all. Knowledge shared freely comes back multiplied.
V
Quality is the brand
Never ship work you would not be proud to show a new client. Mediocre work delivered fast is always worse than great work delivered a day late. Quality is how Lextech earns its reputation and every single one of its future contracts.
VI
No private client dealing
No team member approaches Lextech clients privately for personal business while working here. No member recruits teammates away. Both are fundamental trust violations and grounds for immediate separation from the company — no exceptions.
VII
Respect is non-negotiable
No condescension, no dismissiveness, no bullying in any form. We are a team of peers. Disagreement is welcome and healthy; disrespect is never tolerated. This applies to how we treat every colleague and every client we encounter.
VIII
Every problem is everyone's problem
When a teammate is stuck, help them. When a client is unhappy, everyone rallies around resolving it. We do not work in silos. A problem on any project is a Lextech problem — not just one person's burden to carry alone.
IX
Continuous learning is expected
Technology moves fast. Every team member is expected to be actively learning something new relevant to their role at all times. Lextech invests in this learning — but the internal drive must come from within each person.
X
Represent Lextech everywhere
How you behave at a client site, at a tech event, or even on social media reflects on the company and everyone in it. Be professional, be helpful, and be someone that Lextech is genuinely proud to have representing it.

"Print this and put it on the wall. Review it as a team every quarter. The code of conduct only works if it is lived daily — not just written once and forgotten."

06
Operations
The first 90 days determine whether Lextech becomes a company or stays a hustle. Build the foundation right and everything else becomes easier.

Build systems early. They are what make a startup into a company. Every time you do something manually twice, ask: how do I build this once so it runs without me?

First 6 Critical Problems to Solve

1
Formalize the business immediately
Register Lextech with the Registrar General's Department. Get a TIN. Open a dedicated business bank account. Without this, large clients, NGOs, and government entities cannot pay you. Takes 1–2 weeks and costs under GHS 500. Do it before pitching anyone.
2
Build Lextech's own digital presence first
A stunning website, active LinkedIn, professional email (name@lextech.com.gh), and a portfolio page — all before pitching your first client. You cannot credibly sell web design services with a poor-quality website. This is non-negotiable.
3
Create contract and proposal templates
You need a standard service agreement, a proposal template, and an invoice template from day one. Without written agreements, clients exploit vague promises. Start with Notion or Google Docs and have a lawyer review your standard contract when you can afford it.
4
Set up your core toolstack in week one
Notion or Trello for project management. GitHub for code. Figma for design. Google Workspace for email and documents. WhatsApp Business for client communication. Paystack or Hubtel for collecting payments. Get these in place before you need them.
5
Land your first 3 paying clients
Everything else is noise until money comes in. Focus the first 30 days entirely on finding 3 paying clients — even small ones. Case studies, cash flow, and team confidence all come from this. Use your personal network first. Offer a modest first-client discount to break resistance.
6
Document every process from day one
Every repeating task, every client template, every decision — write it down in Notion. When you hire your 5th person, they should onboard themselves from your documentation without you explaining everything manually. This is how a startup becomes a real company.

Notion as the central hub for everything. Linear or Trello for active development sprints. Every project has a Notion page from kickoff to delivery and beyond.

Problem-Solving Protocol

When anything goes wrong — client complaint, missed deadline, technical failure — follow this sequence every time without exception:

  • Acknowledge immediately. Contact whoever is affected within the hour. Do not go silent under any circumstances.
  • Diagnose honestly. What actually went wrong? Gather facts before jumping to conclusions or assigning blame.
  • Own it completely. Even if others contributed. Lextech's name is on every piece of work that leaves this company.
  • Present a fix with a specific timeline. Not just an apology — what exactly will you do and by precisely when?
  • Deliver the fix. Then follow up to confirm it fully resolved the issue from the client's perspective.
  • Document and change the process. Capture what happened and update the system so the same failure cannot repeat.

Growth Strategy
From your first client to a company that builds itself. The goal is to gradually shift from founder-dependent to system-dependent.

The company that builds itself is built on systems, not heroics. Every time you do something manually twice, ask: how do I build this once so it runs without me? Processes, templates, playbooks. That is how you scale without burning out.

The 3-Layer Growth Engine

Layer 1 — Foundation
Referral Engine
Do work so good clients recommend you without being asked — but ask anyway. At the end of every project: "Do you know 2 business owners with similar challenges?" Referrals cost nothing and convert at 3x the rate of cold outreach.
Layer 2 — Amplification
Content Authority
Post twice weekly on LinkedIn about tech problems you solve for Ghanaian businesses. Topics like "5 ways SMEs in Ghana lose money without a proper website" reach exactly your target clients. Consistency over 12 months builds inbound leads that arrive already trusting you.
Layer 3 — Scale
SaaS Product
Pick one vertical where you see repeated pain across multiple clients. Build a focused SaaS product. 50 subscribers at GHS 800/month = GHS 40,000/month in pure recurring revenue with no additional sales effort. This is where Lextech scales.
Multiplier
Strategic Partnerships
Build relationships with accountants, consultants, and hardware vendors who serve your exact target clients daily. A referral partnership turns one person's network into Lextech's pipeline. Pay a referral fee — it always pays for itself with the very first client sent.

LinkedIn Content Strategy

Post twice per week, every week, without fail. Consistency over 12 months is worth infinitely more than one viral post. Topics that perform well:

Problem-solution posts
A specific problem a Ghanaian business faces, followed by how technology solves it. Relatable, practical, and directly attracts your ideal clients who recognize themselves in the problem.
Behind-the-scenes
Show the team working, show a project in progress, show a client win. Humanizes Lextech and builds trust before a potential client ever contacts you for the first time.
Local case studies
One client problem, your solution, the result. Even small results matter. "We helped a Lapaz pharmacy track stock and saved them GHS 5,000 in losses" gets shared by the exact people you want to reach.
Tech education for non-tech owners
Simple explanations of tech concepts written for Ghanaian business owners who are not technical. They share what makes them look informed to their peers, and Lextech becomes their trusted expert.

Partnerships That Accelerate Growth

  • Computer and hardware shops in Accra — Mutual referral agreement. They send IT clients to you; you send hardware buyers to them. Zero cost, immediate upside.
  • Business consultants and accountants — They work with every SME in your target market. A 10% referral fee on every project they send you pays back within a single converted client.
  • Digital agencies needing development capacity — Become their trusted overflow tech partner while building your own independent client base in parallel.
  • Ghana Chamber of Commerce / GIPC — Show up to events. Sponsor a session. Speak on a tech panel. Get in front of 50 business owners in a single evening for the cost of a sponsorship.
  • Google Workspace and Microsoft 365 reseller programs — Official partner status gives you credibility, commission on every subscription, and a concrete reason to walk into any business.
  • University career offices — Position Lextech as a partner for student internships. Get skilled junior talent and visibility in the best talent pools in Ghana simultaneously.
